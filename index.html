Professional, Long-Distance Moving Companies: The Basics

Moving companies provide full-service relocation for residential and commercial customers. A professional moving company will typically pack, load, transport, unload, unpack, and store your belongings for you.

Whether you're looking for interstate or international relocation services, our list above provides trustworthy and reliable recommendations to take the stress out of moving.

The movers featured on our list are considered “relocation brokers.” Moving brokers are in charge of organizing every part of a customer’s move: who packs the belongings, who transports the belongings, who unpacks the belongings, and more. Instead of staffing every move in every part of the country on their own, moving brokers rely on a network of transportation specialists based throughout the country to perform the physical tasks of long-distance relocation.

Commonly Provided Services

Long-distance moving companies do more than simply haul your stuff. Most moving companies will offer services such as:

  • Provide the Packing Materials
  • Pack Up Your Belongings
  • Transport Your Belongings: By truck, boat, or air.
  • Unpack Your Belongings
  • Store Your Belongings: Professional movers will typically offer both short- and long-term storage solutions.
  • Transport Special or Hard-to-Move Items: Most movers provide transportation services for traditionally hard-to-relocate items, including vehicles, pianos, or priceless art.

Pricing

Every move consists of unique factors that affect cost. For the most accurate pricing information for your specific relocation, it’s best to get a few quotes.

After reading through our list of professional, long-distance movers, we recommend requesting a quote from your favorite companies. Quotes are free and only take a few minutes to request.

Why You Should Hire Professionals for Your Next Move

Oftentimes, people looking to relocate with a professional want to:

  • Save Money: In some scenarios, it’s actually the same price or cheaper for an individual to relocate with the help of a professional rather than moving DIY-style.
  • Save Time: Packing, planning transportation routes, moving boxes in and out of a rented vehicle: a DIY move can be time-consuming. A professional mover does most of the work for you, so you can continue on with your typical schedule.
  • Avoid Strain: Moving can be extremely hard on the body. Customers that are prone to injury or are physically unable to lift heavy furniture and drive long distances may need the help of professionals.
  • Get Help with Red Tape: For customers moving their belongings out of the state—and especially for customers moving out of the country—there are numerous legal and customs barriers that have to be considered. Professional movers can offer time-tested advice to make the red tape disappear.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How Early Should I Book a Professional Mover?
The golden rule of booking a professional move is to book ASAP. If you’re in a bit of a pinch, most interstate moves can be booked two or three months in advance. For international moves, you should book at least six months in advance.
How Do I Choose the Right Moving Company?
The easiest way to decide on a moving company is to break the process into two steps. First, click through the moving companies included on our list and see which appeal to your relocation needs. Secondly, get and compare quotes from your list of favorite companies. After comparing quotes, you’ll see which company offers you the best deal for your situation.
Where Can I Move to with a Professional Mover?
The moving companies on our list will relocate customers from one state to another, or from the United States to another country.
Are Professional Moving Companies Insured in the Case of an Incident?
Most of the moving companies on our list are insured. Please see the company websites listed above for more information.
